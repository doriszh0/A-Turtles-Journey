Examples of Endangered Marine Species


Hawksbill Turtle:
Habitat: coral reefs, tropical & subtropical waters
Diet: sea sponges, coral, fish, jellyfish
Lifespan: ~50+ years
Importance: keep the coral reef healthy by controlling sea sponge growth
Why they’re endangered:
  • Coastal development that has reduced the size of their nesting grounds
  • Domestic land creatures destroying their nests
  • Eggs harvested for food
  • Hunted for shells
  • Deterioration of the coral reef (main source of food and habitat)
  • Unintentionally fall prey to fishing nets
    • Blue Whale:
    Habitat: open ocean
    Diet: krill
    Lifespan: ~80-90 years
    Importance: their fecal matter releases nutrients that phytoplankton need to survive, helping contribute to the production of half the world’s oxygen; their carcasses also create a unique ecosystem that feed many bottom dwellers
    Why they’re endangered:
  • Noise pollution (sonar)
  • Motor accidents
  • Commercial whaling
  • Tangled in fish nets → prevents them from getting air and/or food
    • Elkhorn Coral:
    Habitat: Coral Reef
    Diet: plankton, sugars from algae
    Lifespan: hundreds of years
    Importance: gives the shore storm cover, offers grounds for tourism, source for disease-curing medicines, provides habitat, nesting grounds
    Why they’re endangered:
  • Ocean acidification
  • Global warming → coral bleaching
  • Disease
  • Pollution
  • Dredging
  • habitat destruction
  • Overharvesting
  • Overfishing
    • Whale Shark:
    Habitat: tropical oceans
    Diet: plankton and fish eggs
    Lifespan: 70+ years
    Importance: indicator species (presence indicates healthy level of plankton and overall ocean health)
    Why they’re endangered:
  • Bycatch
  • Hunted for meat, fins, and oil
  • Injuries from boat accidents
    • Bluefin Tuna:
    Habitat: open ocean
    Diet: zooplankton (when young), fish, and other bony invertebrates
    Lifespan: up to 40 years
    Importance: They are apex predators so losing them will upset the ecosystem’s balance
    Why they’re endangered:
  • Overfishing
